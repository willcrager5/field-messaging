# Banned Phrases — Master Enforcement List

**This is the single source of truth for all vocabulary enforcement across Field's brand.**
Load this file at the start of every session. Every item applies to every surface, every skill, every output.
No exceptions. If a word or pattern appears in input or output, flag it and replace it.

---

## Part 1 — Hard-Ban Word List

Remove or replace every word below. Use short, concrete language instead.
If the meaning is important, name the specific thing. If it isn't, cut it.

## Part 2 — Hard-Ban Expression Patterns

Detect exact and loose matches. Rewrite using the approved alternatives.

---

### A. The "In a World Where" Drama

**Formula:** "In a world where [scary/overwhelming change], [virtue/edge] becomes [currency/advantage]."

**Flag when you see:** "In a world where..." / "In today's fast-paced world..." / "In an era where..."

**Use instead:**
- Today, [specific change]. So [practical implication for the reader].
- Because [concrete change], do [specific action] to get [specific result].

---

### B. The "Most People vs. Few Who" Split

**Formula:** "Most people [lazy thing]. The few who win [disciplined thing]."

**Flag when you see:** "Most people X. But the ones who Y..." / "Most [group] do X. The best do Y."

**Use instead:**
- Among [group], [specific stat/observation]. What works: [one tactic].
- Personal stance: I do [specific practice] to avoid [specific downside].

---

### C. The "Stop Doing X. Start Doing Y." Switch

**Formula:** "Stop [old habit]. Start [new habit]."

**Flag when you see:** "Stop X. Start Y." / "Quit X. Do Y instead."

**Use instead:**
- If you're doing [X], switch to [Y] when [condition], because [reason].
- Replace [X] with [Y]: [1–3 concrete steps the reader can take].

---

### D. The "Not This. Not That. But This." Triple

**Formula:** "It's not [obvious]. It's not [second obvious]. It's [third 'unexpected']."

**Flag when you see:** Three-part "It's not / It's not / It's" construction.

**Use instead:**
- Make one clear claim: "[single point]." Because: [evidence/example].
- Comparison with criteria: Choose [B] over [A] when [condition], since [reason].

---

### E. The "If You're Not Doing X, You're Already Behind" FOMO

**Formula:** "If you're not [using/doing X] yet, you're already [losing/behind]."

**Flag when you see:** "If you're not X yet..." / "You're already behind if you haven't..."

**Use instead:**
- If [goal], start with [small first step]. It takes [time] and yields [measurable benefit].
- Option trade-off: You can skip [X]; you give up [specific upside] and keep [specific cost].

---

### F. The "The Real Work Is…" Reveal

**Formula:** "The real [work/game/battle] isn't [what everyone sees]. It's [what 'masters' do]."

**Flag when you see:** "The real X isn't Y. It's Z." / "Most people focus on X. The real issue is Y."

**Use instead:**
- Spell the work out: [Step 1], [Step 2], [Step 3], with [tool/time/criteria].
- Show backstage with numbers: "[task] takes [time], costs [amount], judged by [criteria]."

---

### G. The "You Don't Need More X. You Need Y." Minimalist Smack

**Formula:** "You don't need more [resources]. You need [intangible virtue]."

**Flag when you see:** "You don't need more X. You need Y." / "It's not about having more X."

**Use instead:**
- Thresholds: Cap [tools/resources] at [N]. Spend [time/frequency] on [specific action].
- Checklist to act: To reach [goal], do: [item 1], [item 2], [item 3] weekly.

---

### H. The "It's Never Been Easier / Harder" Paradox

**Formula:** "It's never been easier to [do thing]. It's never been harder to [do deeper thing]."

**Flag when you see:** "Never been easier to X. Never been harder to Y." / any "it's never been [superlative]" pairing.

**Use instead:**
- Trade-off: [Friction A] dropped, but [constraint B] rose. Handle it by [action].
- Scope it: For [audience/context], [task] is easier due to [factor], and results still depend on [specific].

---

### I. The "Here's the Truth / Nobody Tells You" Fake Reveal

**Formula:** "Here's the truth: [obvious statement]." / "What nobody tells you is that [obvious statement]."

**Flag when you see:** "Here's the truth:" / "What nobody tells you" / "The dirty secret is" / "Here's what most people miss:"

**Use instead:**
- State the fact directly with detail: "[specific assertion + detail]."
- Evidence first: "[data/anecdote]. So [implication/action]."

---

### J. The "It Is Not This. It Is That." Binary

**Formula:** "It is [not this]. It is [that]." — two-part binary claim used as a rhetorical device.

**Flag when you see:** "It is not X. It is Y." / "This is not about X. It's about Y." as a standalone rhetorical move.

**Use instead:**
- Make the one true claim directly: "[What it is], because [reason]."
- If contrast is necessary, name both sides with criteria: "X is [description]. Y is [description]. For [situation], [choice] because [reason]."

---

### K. Em Dashes as Rhetorical Decoration

**Flag when you see:** Em dashes (—) used to add drama, create a pause effect, or introduce a "reveal" at the end of a sentence. Pattern: "X, Y, Z — [punchy conclusion]."

**Use instead:**
- A period. Let the sentence stand on its own.
- A comma, if the clause genuinely belongs in the same sentence.
- Em dashes are acceptable for a true parenthetical aside (replacing parentheses). They are not acceptable as a drama device.

## Part 3 — Style Rules (Apply to All Output)

These rules apply whenever generating or reviewing content.

- **Varied sentence structure and length.** Short sentences after long ones. Not every sentence the same length.
- **Everyday words.** If a simpler word exists, use it. Don't dress up plain ideas.
- **Direct statements over moralizing.** State the thing. Don't preach about it.
- **No hype tone.** No exclamation marks in body copy. No inflated superlatives. Claims land harder when they're stated plainly.
- **Preserve the original POV.** Keep I/you/we as written. Keep tense. Keep the ideas, claims, and structure unless the structure itself is a banned pattern.
- **Don't invent.** No new stories, numbers, or promises that weren't in the source.
- **Same length.** Don't expand or compress significantly when editing. Rewrite, don't rewrite the scope.
